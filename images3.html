Homework 4: Photomontage

What does it mean to be American? It is a question that has recently come under fire considering the various concerns for American’s alienable rights, safety, and ability to practice freedom of speech. Is it the residence? citizenship? Birthright? Or even, the legality of birthright citizenship? The technicalities is something one can argue for, yet, when someone joins in on a rally for America’s affordability, job availability, health insurance, and their neighbor’s safety, is the question of their legal status even a question? Aren’t their sentiments for sharing the collective’s fight for affordable residence, grocery prices, and health affordability the blaring point? While many would argue the latter, others may find comfort in a country that is the most polarized it has ever been, there are still shared sentiments over those who live in it.

Hence, I made this photomontage as an ode to the shared sentiments of all those who make it a concern for the livelihoods of not just themselves, but their neighbors as well. I have used various masking methods to play with color in order to emphasize patriotism in a picture of an ICE rally. I emphasize them in blue, to hint at the collective fight that is, by definition of sentiments, is democratically American. Three other pictures are then meticulously blended over it with hints of red, so as to represent the various actors that both question and validate the rally's patriotism. Yet the two modes coexist, in one picture, circling back to questions of what it means to be American as the photomontage presents the vague framing in its definition. I call it, “The American Flag”.
